\abstractzh{
本文聚焦新兴市场情境下资本市场自由化能否因果性推动企业数字化转型。本文以中国A股分阶段纳入MSCI新兴市场指数为制度性冲击，该冲击提升了样本企业面向全球投资者的可得性与关注度，构成具有政策含义的准自然实验。基于2010--2022年2,712家上市公司、35,264个公司-年份观测值，本文采用双向固定效应双重差分（DID）识别策略，并结合事件研究、倾向得分匹配双重差分（PSM-DID）、安慰剂检验与分期处理诊断开展稳健性验证。

实证结果显示，在优选基准模型下，MSCI纳入使企业数字化转型强度提高0.39个对数点，约相当于处理前水平提升48\%。动态事件研究结果表明，处理前不存在系统性趋势差异，处理后效应持续且具有累积特征。进一步地，安慰剂处理、分期处理诊断、替代聚类标准误、受限样本检验与多口径被解释变量结果均支持核心结论。异质性分析发现，该效应在高技术行业和经济较发达地区更为显著，表明吸收能力与制度互补性会影响自由化收益。机制检验对两条可直接识别的渠道提供一致证据：融资约束缓解，以及外部监督与信息披露纪律强化带来的治理改善。总体而言，本文表明国际金融一体化可作为新兴市场企业数字化升级的重要制度催化机制。

\abstracten{
This thesis examines whether capital market liberalization can causally accelerate firm-level digital transformation in an emerging-market setting. The empirical context is the phased inclusion of Chinese A-share firms in the MSCI Emerging Markets Index, a policy-relevant institutional shock that increased selected firms' exposure to global investors. Leveraging this quasi-natural experiment, the analysis combines identification-focused econometrics with large-scale computational measurement to produce evidence that is both causally interpretable and economically meaningful. The core sample includes 35,264 firm-year observations from 2,712 listed firms over 2010--2022. Treatment effects are estimated using a two-way fixed-effects difference-in-differences design, supplemented by event-study dynamics, matching-based diagnostics, and placebo-based falsification tests.

The baseline results show that MSCI inclusion raises digital transformation intensity by 0.39 log points in the preferred specification, equivalent to roughly a 48\% increase relative to pre-treatment levels. Dynamic event-study estimates reveal no systematic divergence before treatment and persistent effects after treatment, supporting the parallel-trends assumption and pointing to a cumulative adjustment process rather than a one-off disclosure response. A broad set of robustness checks---including placebo assignments, staggered-adoption diagnostics, propensity-score-matched DID, alternative clustering strategies, restricted-sample tests, and multiple outcome definitions---supports this conclusion.

The thesis then moves beyond average effects by examining heterogeneity and transmission mechanisms. Treatment effects are stronger in high-technology industries and economically advanced regions, suggesting that absorptive capacity and institutional complementarity shape the gains from liberalization. Mechanism evidence consistently supports two directly testable channels: financing-constraint relaxation and governance improvement through stronger external monitoring and disclosure discipline. Overall, the findings indicate that international financial integration can function as an institutional catalyst for corporate digital upgrading in emerging markets.
